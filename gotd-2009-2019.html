The first catch is that I think I love long sentences, and I'm very aware that I love ellipses and parenthesized comments.

The second catch is... If you know enough about a game that is not quite perfect and have spent enough time with them, sometimes your love and attention comes through as a detailed and needlessly constructive identification of flaws. This is a habit of mine that I often use as a tool for an exploration of ideas, but it’s completely uncalled for in a celebration of a long, long decade of the vast, vast growth of video games, and they're mostly re-raising issues that the devs are probably already well aware of and couldn't (and probably shouldn't) even address. Given *any* of these games, I absolutely can go for a long time discussing the ins and outs of them. It takes a fine touch and the right context for constructive criticism to be properly constructive, and there's a wide gulf between convincing you that I care about something versus convincing you that you should care that I care about something.

Overall, though, the biggest limitation for any list that I can muster up is whether or not I got around to playing something. It’s rough. If you don't see a game here, it's because I haven't gotten around to it, for some reason or other. ...or I forgot about it. There are a lot of people out there, and therefore there are a lot of games. Even in the depths of the trench of derivative games built for quick money, there is exploration of the space of possible games, and there is often some morsel creativity itching to reach something new.

I also tend to take a long time before I "feel like" playing something. My backlog is huge.
